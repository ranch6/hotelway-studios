# Hotelway Studios — Content & Pitch Playbook

Working doc. Constraint-aware: ~1 shoot weekend per month, full-time job, target
pitch: Hyatt Place Tallinn, March 2026.

---

## 1. Shot-list framework (the "complete stay" arc)

Adapted from the guest-experience approach hospitality photographers use
(shoot the whole stay, not just rooms). Every location — hotel, Airbnb, or your
own apartment styled as one — gets shot as a **sequence**, not a collection:

1. **Arrival** — door, key/keycard, threshold, first step in
2. **The room reveal** — slow push from doorway; bed + window light
3. **The view you wake up to** — curtain pull, window frame, morning light
4. **Morning ritual** — coffee/kettle steam, breakfast details, robe, slippers
5. **Texture pass** — linen close-ups, tile, wood, fixtures, stationery
6. **Passage** — corridors, stairwells, elevator, symmetry shots
7. **Common spaces** — lobby, lounge, pool/spa if present
8. **Day-to-dusk ambiance** — same space at 2–3 times of day (lamps on at dusk = money shot)
9. **Departure beat** — key on the desk, door closing (bookends the arc)

Rule: every shoot produces the full arc. One arc = 1 photo set + 3–5 reels.

## 2. Shooting priorities (first 3 shoot weekends)

With ~1 weekend/month, each weekend must produce a complete portfolio unit:

- **Weekend 1 — Airbnb with great light** (book a design-forward listing, 1 night).
  Cheapest full-control environment. Produces the complete arc + tests your
  editing pipeline end to end.
- **Weekend 2 — NYC hotel day-rate or 1-night stay** (boutique, lobby-forward:
  think Ace/Public/Freehand tier, or a well-designed budget boutique).
  Adds corridors, lobby, elevator — the "hotel-ness" an Airbnb can't give.
- **Weekend 3 — second contrasting property** (moody/dark vs. airy/bright, so
  the portfolio shows range within the same quiet aesthetic).

Also: harvest existing clips from past trips now — anything matching the arc
(doors, windows, breakfast) can be regraded to the house look for the mood reel.

## 3. Reel structures (3 repeatable templates, 15–30s)

All: no faces, no VO, licensed ambient/quiet audio, minimal text (thin serif,
lowercase, 2–4 words max), slow movement, cut on motion.

**A. "Check in with me" (arrival arc)** — the workhorse
- 0–2s: door/key detail (hook: threshold anticipation)
- 2–6s: door opens, slow push into room
- 6–12s: bed + window light, curtain pull to view
- 12–18s: 3 quick texture details (1.5s each)
- 18–24s: wide settle shot, lamp on
- Text: "checking into [place]" → nothing else until end card

**B. "Slow morning at [place]" (ritual arc)**
- Steam/coffee pour → window light → breakfast detail → robe/slippers →
  view wide. 5 shots, 3–5s each, all at golden/soft morning light.

**C. "The details you miss" (texture arc)**
- 8–10 macro/detail shots, 1.5–2s each, rhythmic cuts to quiet audio.
  Cheapest to shoot (one room, one hour), performs well, very brandable.

Editing pipeline: harmony-tool covers trim, speed ramp, color grade, caption
overlay, social crop, watermark, frame-grab (stills from video = bonus photos).

## 4. Credibility without clients (do / don't)

Do:
- Frame early work as a **"Studio Series"** — self-initiated location studies,
  published monthly. Self-initiated ≠ fake; studios do this openly.
- Keep the mood board section clearly labeled as references (already done).
- Write 1 short case-study page per shoot weekend: 3 stills, 1 reel, 100 words
  on approach. Three of these reads as a real body of work.
- Get the Instagram grid consistent before pitching — hotels will check IG
  before the website. 12 posts in the house style is the credibility threshold.
- Put process language everywhere ("one quiet day on property, zero
  disruption") — operational empathy reads as experience.

Don't:
- Fake client logos or imply commissions that didn't happen.
- Pad with unlabeled inspiration images in the Work section.
- Launch with "we/our team" claims you can't back — "small studio" is honest
  and on-brand.

## 5. Pitch playbook — Hyatt Place Tallinn, March 2026

NOTE: the brief says "March 2026," but that date has passed (it is now
July 2026) — assuming the real target is **March 2027**. Working backward:

- **T-8 months (Jul 2026, now):** 3 completed studio-series shoots live on site + IG.
- **T-6 months:** soft outreach — follow/engage with the property's IG,
  identify the marketing contact (property-level, not corporate).
- **T-4 months:** send pitch (see below). Offer is content-for-stay: 1–2 night
  stay → they receive a defined deliverable pack (e.g., 3 reels + 15 stills,
  organic usage included, paid usage priced separately).
- **T-2 months:** confirm dates, send shot list + one-pager, book travel.

Pitch email skeleton (keep under 150 words):
1. One line: who you are + the one thing you do.
2. One line: why *their* property (name a specific design/light feature).
3. The offer: exact deliverables, exact ask (nights), zero cost framing.
4. Link: site + 2 best reels. No attachments.
5. One-line close with a specific proposed window.

Why this works for a Hyatt Place: select-service hotels have small/no content
budgets but active social accounts — free professional content for a
comped room is an easy internal yes for a property GM/marketing lead.
